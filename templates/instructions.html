Green Pass Compliant Facilities:

  • Culture and sporting events, including marathons. If a seated sports event, and no more than 50 people indoors or 1000 people outdoors - no green pass required
  • Prayer events held in seated attendance only and with marked seats, indoors up to 50 people and outdoors up to 1,000 peopleâ€“ No Green Pass required.
  • Places of worship with more than 50 people
  • Cinemas and theatres
  • Recreational establishments and clubs
  • Conferences and exhibitions
  • Hotels: Hotel check-ins are possible with a negative PCR test result test if taken outside of Israel no more than 72 hours before arrival in a hotel
  • Indoor or outdoor hotel, hostel, or guest house pools. A Green Pass is not mandated in other indoor or outdoor pools (which are in hotels), but Purple Badge restrictions apply
  • Gyms and studios
  • Event venues and gardens
  • Festivals
  • Restaurants, bars, dining rooms and cafes. The Green Pass requirement applies to the indoor area of a restaurant and not to the outdoor area
  • Museums: Students and chaperons without a Green Pass can participate if in the same formation as at school (regular capsules) while maintaining maximum distance between students and other museum visitors
  • Libraries: The Green Pass is not required of visitors who only come to take books
  • Indoor tourist attractions and amusement parks. The Green Pass requirement does not apply to outdoor attractions
  • Universities and establishments of higher learning
  • Practical driving tests
  • Employees in healthcare and social services facilities.
  • Guided tours for children or teenagers from an orange or red locality in an informal education setting (such as a youth movement).
